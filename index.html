Artifact 2: Visualizing Neural Networks

Title

Visualizing Neural Networks

Introduction

This artifact explains the structure and function of neural network components using a visual slide presentation. It shows how data flows from the input layer through hidden layers to the output layer, and it explains how the model learns through weights, activation functions, loss functions, and optimization algorithms.

Description

The artifact is a visual presentation created to simplify neural network concepts for students and early AI learners. It includes diagrams, component definitions, training workflow, and a summary of insights gained from exploring Neural Network Playground.

Objective

The objective of this artifact is to clearly define and describe neural network components, visually illustrate how they work together, and communicate complex AI concepts in a concise and professional way.

Process

I reviewed the assignment requirements, practiced with Neural Network Playground, identified the required neural network components, created a visual structure showing input, hidden, and output layers, and explained each component using clear language. I then organized the content into a slide-style presentation suitable for my professional portfolio.

Tools and Technologies Used

  • PowerPoint
  • LaTeX Beamer
  • TikZ
  • Neural Network Playground
  • Course resources
  • GitHub Pages

Value Proposition

This artifact helps students and early AI learners understand neural network components through clear visuals and concise explanations. It demonstrates my ability to simplify complex AI concepts, connect theory to practice, and communicate technical ideas professionally.

Unique Value

The unique value of this artifact is that it combines visual diagrams with plain-language explanations. It does not only define neural network terms; it shows how the components work together during training.

Relevance

This artifact is relevant because neural networks are used in image recognition, language processing, prediction, and many modern AI systems. Understanding their components is important for learning how AI models are built and trained.

Artifact 3: Training Generative AI Large Language Models

Title

Training Generative AI Large Language Models

Introduction

This artifact explains the infrastructure and resource costs required to train advanced generative AI large language models. It uses GPT-4 and Claude as two major examples to show how data, compute power, energy, training time, and human feedback influence model performance, scalability, and cost.

Description

The artifact is a slide-based infographic presentation that visualizes the end-to-end LLM training pipeline, from data collection and cleaning to tokenization, distributed training, alignment, evaluation, and deployment. It also compares GPT-4 and Claude to show how different AI systems require major infrastructure investments.

Objective

The objective of this artifact is to clearly communicate how generative AI models are trained and why training these systems is expensive. It highlights the role of datasets, GPU/TPU infrastructure, energy usage, time, and human evaluation in building modern AI systems.

Process

I reviewed course resources on large language models and researched how advanced AI systems are trained. I then narrowed the artifact to a deeper comparison of GPT-4 and Claude so the presentation would be more focused and easier to understand. I organized the content into a professional visual format using diagrams, tables, and concise explanations.

Tools and Technologies Used

  • PowerPoint
  • Course resources
  • Research on GPT-4 and Claude training approaches
  • GitHub Pages

Value Proposition

This artifact helps AI students and early technology professionals understand the hidden infrastructure behind generative AI systems. It demonstrates my ability to simplify complex AI concepts, compare real-world models, and communicate technical cost drivers clearly.

Unique Value

The unique value of this artifact is that it does not only describe what LLMs do; it explains what it takes to build and operate them. It connects technical training steps to real cost categories such as data, compute, energy, time, and human feedback.

Relevance

This artifact is relevant because generative AI systems are becoming widely used in education, business, software development, and decision support. Understanding their infrastructure helps explain both their power and their limitations.
